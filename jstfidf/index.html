    This exploratory tool graphs words from presidential inaugurals according to their frequency in each work and across the whole corpus.

    The vertical axis represents inverse document frequency on a log scale, so words in all documentsd) are always on the bottom row; words that appear only in the current document are always on the top row. The horizontal axis represents term frequency in the current document, also on a log scale. When changing from one document to another words may appear or disappear, and may move right or left, but they will always be at essentially the same height, because that value is determined by the corpus rather than the document. (For the sake of clarity, however, circles and labels are randomly jittered just a little.)

    TF-IDF is a calculation sometimes used in information retrieval that ranks words in a document highly when they are both frequent in a document and appear in relatively few other documents in the corpus. Conventionally TF-IDF assigns each word a single score, but this scatterplot separates out the two parts of the calculation to plot all terms in two dimensions. Words with the highest TF-IDF scores appear at the upper right.

    A faint diagonal line shows the slope of relations of TF-IDF equivalency. Words with higher TF-IDF scores can found above and to the right of the line, wherever it is.
